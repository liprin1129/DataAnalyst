1. What is our independent variable? What is our dependent variable?¶

Independent variable: This is a variable that can be operated for an experiment so as to see the influences on a dependent variable. It is also called experimental or predictor variable. Therefore, in the Stroop task, the independent variable would be congruent and incongruent conditions. For both conditions, colours and colour-word pairs can be varied. Also, participants can be grouped into ages, their jobs in order to observe more detailed effects on those groups.

Dependent variable: This is occasionally called an outcome variable, and for the Stroop task, it would be the time that the participants pronounce colours of the inks on both tasks.

2. What is an appropriate set of hypotheses for this task? What kind of statistical test do you expect to perform? Justify your choices.¶

In the human brain, the information process for a reading alphabet will have a priority than that for a colour information. In other words, processing a colour information in the human brain will have more steps than reading alphabets, then people will spend more time to say out "the colours of the inks in which the word is printed" on incongruent words than that on congruent words.

Using hypothesis text, it can be determined that how a large amount of effects on a sample of data would have a certain condition on the entire population.

$\mu_C$ denotes the mean (or parameter) of the congruent words population, and $\mu_I$ represents the mean (or parameter) of the incongruent words population.

Null Hypothesis $H_0: \mu_I=\mu_C$, which means people will not be affected by incongruent words task.

Alternative Hypothesis $H_a: \mu_I > \mu_C$, which means that people tend to have more time to finish incongruent words task than congruent words task.

There are 24 samples for each dataset from the population. For the hypothesis test:

  • t-statistics will be used to identify this hypothesis test.
  • It is assumed that the samples follows the central limit theorem, so the distributions are Gaussian.
  • This test will be a paired t test, because the samples are involved with repeated measures, such as pre- and post-test. Both samples are aquired from arbitrary people, and they do not have influence to each other.

There are 24 sample data in each test. If $\mathbf{x}$ is a congruent dataset and $\mathbf{y}$ is incongruent dataset, the mean of the congruent dataset, $\bar{\mathbf{x}}\approx \mu_C$, is 14.04 with standard deviation of the sample, $s_\mathbf{x}=\frac{\sum \mathbf{x}}{n_x-1}=3.56$, and $\bar{\mathbf{y}}\approx \mu_I=22.02$ with standard deviation of the sample, $s_\mathbf{y}=\frac{\sum \mathbf{y}}{n_y-1}=4.80$.

People tend to take more time to finish the task on the incongruent words than that on the congruent words.   • The mean difference, $\bar{\mathbf{d}}$, is $7.96$.
  • The standard deviation of the differences, $s_d$, is $\sqrt{\frac{\sum(d_i-\mathbf{\bar{d}})^2}{n-1}}=4.86$.

  • Because the degree of freedom $n-1$ is 23, $t_{critical}$ in positive directional one-tailed having at $\alpha=0.05$ is $1.714$.
  • $t_{statistics}=\frac{\bar{\mathbf{d}}}{SE(d)}$ is $8.04$, this is in $t_{critical}$ region.
